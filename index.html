The Devil Wears Prada 2 — Miranda Is Back. And She's Not Playing.

By Kulture Editorial · April 2026 · 4 min read
👠

Almost 20 years later, Miranda Priestly is back — and the fashion world is already shaking. The Devil Wears Prada 2 hits theaters on May 1, 2026, reuniting the original dream cast with the same director and writer who made the first film a cultural milestone.

"Took you long enough." — Miranda Priestly, TDWP2 Trailer

The Story

This time around, the story hits differently. Runway Magazine is in decline — print media is dying, advertising budgets are shrinking, and Miranda Priestly is facing her biggest enemy yet: irrelevance. Her former assistant Emily Charlton, now a powerhouse executive at a luxury brand, holds the key to Runway's survival. The two women, once boss and subordinate, are now rivals fighting for the same dollar.

Andy Sachs returns too — stepping back into the orbit of the woman who changed her life, for better or worse. The trailer shows her holding the elevator for Miranda, the same way everything started. Full circle.

The Cast

Meryl Streep, Anne Hathaway, Emily Blunt and Stanley Tucci all reprise their iconic roles. But this sequel brings serious new energy — Kenneth Branagh joins as Miranda's husband, and Simone Ashley, Justin Theroux, Lucy Liu, and Pauline Chalamet bring fresh faces to the Runway world. Even Lady Gaga shows up, performing an original track called "Runway" alongside Doechii.

Why It Matters

Beyond the fashion, the sequels tackles something real — the collapse of print media, the tension between legacy and relevance, and what happens when powerful women are forced to compete instead of collaborate. It's sharp, it's stylish, and it's exactly the kind of cultural moment Kulture was built to cover.

ElGrande Toto — The King of Moroccan Rap Isn't Slowing Down.

By Kulture Editorial · April 2026 · 5 min read
🎤

If you need one name to understand what Moroccan rap sounds like in 2026, it's ElGrande Toto. Born Taha Fahssi on August 3, 1996 in Casablanca, he went from writing lyrics at age 11 in the BNJ neighborhood to becoming the most streamed Arab artist in the MENA region on Spotify — with over 1 billion streams across 178 countries.

"Caméléon" — because no two tracks ever sound the same. That's the Toto formula.

From BNJ to the World

Growing up in Benjdia, a working-class district in central Casablanca, Toto didn't have a straight path to music. He worked as a teleconsultant for French companies, got rejected for a visa, and turned to rap as a way to tell his story. His aunt in France sent him rap records. He listened. He wrote. He waited.

The 2017 BNJ City Block mixtape launched him. By 2021, his album Caméléon was dominating streaming platforms and earning respect from European heavyweights like Damso and Hamza. He switches between French and Darija seamlessly — a bilingual voice for a generation that lives between two worlds.

The Discography

Three albums deep, each one bolder than the last. Caméléon (2021) established his range. 27 (2023) — a deeply personal project named after a fire that changed his life — hit 6 million streams in its first 24 hours. His third album, SALGOAT (2024), turned a political insult into a brand identity, charting globally within a week of release.

The Brand

SALGOAT isn't just an album — it's a clothing line. Toto turned the word "salgout" (voyou in Darija, used against him by a Moroccan government official) into a streetwear brand. That's the move. That's the culture. Taking what's thrown at you and making it yours.

Under contract with RCA Records (Sony Music France), signed to one of the biggest labels in the world, ElGrande Toto remains the most important voice in Moroccan music right now. And he's only 29.
