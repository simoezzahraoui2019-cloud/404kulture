Lily-Rose Depp — Beyond the Name.

By Kulture Editorial  ·  April 2026  ·  5 min read
💎
'" />

Lily-Rose Depp has grown into one of the most fascinating young performers working in film today — blending old-school Hollywood lineage with a modern, fearless artistic identity that is entirely her own.

"She isn't afraid to take creative risks or step into controversial roles. That's exactly the kind of energy culture needs right now."

Born Into the Scene

Born to actor Johnny Depp and French singer-actress Vanessa Paradis, she grew up between Los Angeles and Paris surrounded by creativity, cinema, music, and fashion. But despite her famous background, Lily-Rose has steadily worked to prove that she's more than just a recognizable last name — building a career defined by bold choices and a quiet confidence that sets her apart.

From Small Roles to Bold Statements

She first stepped into the film world with a small role in the 2014 horror film Tusk, directed by Kevin Smith. From there, she gravitated toward projects that leaned more artistic and character-driven. The King, where she starred alongside Timothée Chalamet, showcased her ability to fit seamlessly into historical dramas while still bringing subtle emotional depth.

The Chanel Icon

Outside of film, Lily-Rose quickly became a defining face in the fashion world. As a longtime ambassador for Chanel, she has fronted major campaigns and appeared at some of the biggest fashion events in the world. Her style carries a mix of Parisian elegance and modern edge — making her one of the most recognizable young fashion icons of her generation.

The Idol & Taking Risks

Her performance in HBO's The Idol placed her at the center of one of the most talked-about shows in pop culture, proving she isn't afraid to take creative risks or step into controversial roles. Whether audiences loved it or debated it, the role demonstrated her willingness to dive into complex characters rather than play it safe.

Lily-Rose Depp is not a celebrity coasting on a last name. She is an artist in the truest sense — choosing projects that challenge her, styling herself with intention, and building a body of work that will outlast any headline.

The Devil Wears Prada 2 — Miranda Is Back. And She's Not Playing.

By Kulture Editorial  ·  April 2026  ·  4 min read

Almost 20 years later, Miranda Priestly is back — and the fashion world is already shaking. The Devil Wears Prada 2 hits theaters on May 1, 2026, reuniting the original dream cast with the same director and writer who made the first film a cultural milestone.

"Took you long enough." — Miranda Priestly, TDWP2 Trailer

The Story

This time around, the story hits differently. Runway Magazine is in decline — print media is dying, advertising budgets are shrinking, and Miranda Priestly faces her biggest enemy yet: irrelevance. Her former assistant Emily Charlton, now a powerhouse executive at a luxury brand, holds the key to Runway's survival. Two women, once boss and subordinate, now rivals fighting for the same dollar.

Andy Sachs returns too — stepping back into Miranda's orbit. The trailer shows her holding the elevator for Miranda, the same way everything started. Full circle.

The Cast

Meryl Streep, Anne Hathaway, Emily Blunt and Stanley Tucci all reprise their iconic roles. Kenneth Branagh joins as Miranda's husband. Simone Ashley, Justin Theroux, Lucy Liu, and Pauline Chalamet bring fresh faces to the Runway world. Lady Gaga performs an original track called "Runway" with Doechii. Even Donatella Versace has a cameo.

Why It Matters

Beyond the fashion, the sequel tackles something real — the collapse of print media, the tension between legacy and relevance, and what happens when powerful women are forced to compete instead of collaborate. Sharp, stylish, and exactly the kind of cultural moment Kulture was built to cover.

ElGrande Toto — The King of Moroccan Rap Isn't Slowing Down.

By Kulture Editorial  ·  April 2026  ·  5 min read

If you need one name to understand Moroccan rap in 2026, it's ElGrande Toto. Born Taha Fahssi on August 3, 1996 in Casablanca, he went from writing lyrics at 11 in the BNJ neighborhood to becoming the most streamed Arab artist in the MENA region — over 1 billion streams in 178 countries.

"Caméléon" — because no two tracks ever sound the same. That's the Toto formula.

From BNJ to the World

Growing up in Benjdia, a working-class district of Casablanca, Toto didn't have a straight path. He worked as a teleconsultant, got rejected for a visa, and turned to rap. His aunt in France sent him rap records. He listened. He wrote. He waited. The 2017 BNJ City Block mixtape launched everything. By 2021, Caméléon was dominating platforms and earning respect from Damso and Hamza.

Three Albums, One King

Caméléon (2021) established his range. 27 (2023) — named after a fire that changed his life — hit 6 million streams in 24 hours. SALGOAT (2024) turned a political insult into a global brand identity, charting worldwide in its first week.

The Brand

SALGOAT is also a clothing line. Toto took the word "salgout" — used against him by a Moroccan government official — and made it a streetwear brand. That's the move. Under contract with RCA Records (Sony Music France), he's the biggest voice in Moroccan music right now. And he's only 29.
